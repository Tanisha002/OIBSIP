Inspiring books written by Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam:
  • India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium
    • It was published in 1998. He advocated an action plan to develop India into a "knowledge superpower" and a developed nation by 2020. He regarded his work on India's nuclear weapons programme as a way to assert India's place as a future superpower.

  • Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Power within India
    • It was published in 2002. He shares his dream of a nation that is unrivaled, he discusses how he has, from his experience, met such skilled people whose visions can transform the nation.

  • Mission of India: A Vision of Indian Youth
    • It was published in 2005. It has been written with the intention of challenging the Indian youth to bring about a positive change in the country by 2020. their goal to make India one among the five top economic powers in the world by 2020.

  • Reignited: Scientific Pathways to a Brighter Future
    • It was published in 2015. This book goes onto explore the options of robotics, neurosciences, paleontology and other fields which may be able to create a better world for the future generations.

  • Wings of Fire
    • One of the best autobiographies written ever, Wings of Fire, describes Dr Kalaam’s incredible journey of a simple boy from a humble background to a chief scientist who had a major hand behind the Indian Space Research innovations and then later how became the President of India.
Dr. A P J Abdul Kalam

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as APJ Abdul Kalam was born in a Tamil Muslim family on October 15th, 1931. He was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who also served as the 11th president of India. Religion and spirituality were very important to him throughout his life. His desire to read and spend hours studying and learning Mathematics, proved to create a strong foundation in his initial stage of education.

Kalam studied physics and aerospace engineering. After graduating from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1960, he joined the DRDO (Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation) as a scientist and late in the early 1960s, worked with the INCOSPAR (Indian National Committee for Space Research) under Vikram Sarabhai, a renowned space scientist. In 1969, Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he started as a project director of SLV-III, India’s first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle. His dedication led to the development of 4 missile projects and due to this contribution, he earned the title of the ‘Missile Man of India’. Later in 1992, Kalam was appointed as the Scientific Adviser to the Defence Minister and promoted to Principal Scientific Adviser to the Government of India in 1999.

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam is one of India’s most celebrated scientists and was President of the Republic of India from 2002 to 2007. An aeronautical engineer by training, Dr. Kalam played a critical part in developing India’s nuclear weapons program as well as establishing and leading India’s space program. Widely referred to as the "People's President", he returned to his civilian life of education, writing and public service after a single term. He was a recipient of several prestigious awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour.

While delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, Kalam collapsed and died on 27 July 2015, from an apparent cardiac arrest, aged 83. He was a leader who contributed to the youth skilling and development initiative, working tirelessly in his field and directing several projects, APJ Abdul Kalam has always been an inspiration to people in this world. Amongst the great personalities, his struggles and passion have always given the power to dream to millions of others.
